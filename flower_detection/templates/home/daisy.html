Introduction

Daisy is a perennial whose evergreen leaves form a basal tuft or a rosette.Daisy flower is composed of white petals and a yellow center, although the flower can sometimes have a pink or rose color.daisies are most popularly recognized by their attractive flowers, which have a central disc with radiating petals.The daisy flower actually contains two flowers: the petals and the cluster.Mostly they grows in himachal,kashmir,and other himalayan region ,but the gerbera daisy can be grown throughout india in polyhouse farming,across world except antartica they are found on all continents.Seaside daisy is native to the coast of Oregon and California.Daisy flower plant has a prostrate fashion or a growing habit of spreading.Daisy flowers open at dawn and are visited by many small insects.

Etymology

It has whorled appearance in old english it was known as daegeseage means"day's eye"(flower opens up at dawn and closes at dusk). It was first used as given name in 19th century.Also known as Asteraceae comes from the Latin word aster, which means “star.” The name of Bellis Perennis derived from latin words which are bellus, meaning pretty, and perennis, meaning everlasting.

Botany

1.) Evolution

The Daisy is over 4,000 years old . Beginning in as early as 2,200 BC daisies were grown in Egyptian temple gardens .The gerbera daisy was discovered in 1884 in South Africa by Robert jameson.They were originated in Europe and temperate region of Asia .The word "daisy" refers not to one single type of plant, but plants that produce flowers having common characteristics.

2.) Species

There are 7 types of Species English Daisy (Bellis perennis) , Gerbera Daisy (Gerbera jamesonii) , Marguerite Daisy (Argyranthemum frutescens) , Oxeye Daisy (Leucanthemum vulgare) , Painted Daisy (Tanacetum coccineum), Shasta Daisy (Leucanthemum x superbum) , Swan River Daisy (Brachyscome iberidifolia)

3.) Medicinal uses

The plant is mildly astringent, anodyne, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, antitussive, demulcent, digestive, emollient, laxative, expectorant, ophthalmic, tonic and purgative. The daisy’s roots and leaves were traditionally used to heal wounds . wild daisy tea used to treat cough, disorder of liver and kidney ,bronchitis and other inflammation of the upper respiratory tract and swelling, also used as drying agent(astringent),blood purifier.extract of daisy act as wound healer,also in toners,serums, whitening agent daisy extract is common ingredient.

Season to grow

It blooms in spring. It survive in dry and wet habitat, but it prefers well drained soils rich in organic matters and lot. seaside Daisy grows on sand dunes, beaches, and coastal bluffs. Seaside daisy is a perennial herb that grows in lot mats and spreads through wood rhizomes, much like Mexican daisy.daisy usually lives in a controlled temperature at all times. This is the ideal condition for the daisy to survive. Different species of daisies can live in diverse conditions. The daisy is also known as a wild flower and can be seen along highways and forests.
