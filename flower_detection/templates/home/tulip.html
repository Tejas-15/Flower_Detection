Introduction

A Tulip is Flower of the spring-blooming .It blossom in cup-shaped also some have star or bowl form all tulip flowers has 6 petals(smooth,ruffled). The flowers of tulips gained huge popularity across history due to their large size, vibrant colors, striking beauty ,showy and brightly colored; generally red, pink, yellow, or white . They often have a different colored blotch at the base of the tepals (petals and sepals, collectively) .Tulips originally were found in a band stretching from Southern Europe to Central Asia ,it grows in Himachal Pradesh,Uttarakhand and hilly areas of Jammu and Kashmir are perfect for tulip cultivation.Netherland is main producer of tulip(3 billion bulb annually);Tulips were frequently depicted in Dutch Golden Age paintings.In their natural state they are adapted to steppes and mountainous areas with temperate climates.Tulips are also popular in Australia and several festivals are held in September and October, during the Southern Hemisphere's spring.

Etymology

The modern name Tulip was first mentioned in western Europe is latinized version of Turkish word for turban,'tulbend' which ultimately derives from persian'Dulband' meaning round."Turks" used the word "tulipan" to describe the flower.

Botany

1.) Evolution

Cultivation of the tulip began in Iran (Persia), probably in the 10th century.The first tulip appeared in France. Establishment of the first cultivation operations south of Haarlem, especially along the Wagenweg and the Kleine Houtweg. These absolute monopolies would hold onto their positions for about 150 years.The Tulip goes to Europe During the 1500's,Beautiful botanical drawings of tulips,Europeans became plant explorers; Tulipa bononiensis, became very famous.By the 15th century, tulips were among the most prized flowers; becoming the symbol of the Ottomans.In the Ottoman Empire, numerous types of tulips were cultivated and bred, and today 14 species can still be found in Turkey. Central Asia, tulips were cultivated in early 1055. There are about 75 species, and these are divided among four subgenera.

2.) Species

There are several varieties of species tulips that will give you a range of colors, foliage, and height for your beds or rock gardens: Lady Tulip ( Tulipa clusiana )this species tulip produces gorgeous, unique flowers that are pink on the outside and white on the inside with a red or purple star in the center.Many Species tulips will naturalize in Southern US states such as Texas, where winters are too mild for hybridized tulips. Successful varieties include Tulipa clusiana (Lady Tulip), Tulipa praecod (Fire tulip), Tulipa saxatilis (Cretan Tulip), Tulipa sylvestris (Florentine Tulip) and Tulipa orphanidea (Greek Tulip)

Uses

1.) Ornamental plants

The 1000 of hybrid & cultivars addition to original species knowns as botanical tulips and many species of tulips are used as ornamental garden plant(grown for displaying ).It is planted in strategic places in the garden.The tulip is an exclusive garden plant.

2.) Cut flowers

tulip to grow for cut flowers just for show then pull the bulb. unique specialty tulips available to cut flower growers; There are frilly, many-petaled varieties that can pass as garden and double-height blooms that look like two scoops of ice cream. ate 17th century people started using them as garden decorator,soon became major trading product in Holland and other parts of Europe.The interest for flowers was huge and bulbs were sold for high prices.

3.) Medicinal uses

It has diuretic,antiseptic properties best remedy for cough and cold,reduces risk of cancer, Used for sinus pain, fever and headache. tulip is consider as powerhouse for skincare , used in cosmetics as it contain high level of growth factor even if it is picked also gives relief,soothing effect on insect bites, bee stings and also rashes on skin so its a excellent poultice . Warm up 2-4 flowers in hot water. Dip a towel in the hot water and drop the petals of the flowers into the towel. Roll the towel to crush the leaves. Apply the crushed petals to area where there is skin rash, bee sting or insect bite to find quick relief from the irritation. Hold the leaves on the place for 10 minutes using the hot towel.

4.) Health benefits

Tulip also comprise of cosmetic uses. Tulip extracts are used in many beauty products. Cosmetic uses of Tulip are Best for Dry sensitive skin, Used in creams, hand lotions and in essential oils, Used in Perfumes.

5.) Perfumes and food

Scent in tulips is linked to color, due to the species used in the development of the modern hybrids. A lemon-colored species called Tulipa sylvestris is very sweetly scented. Its descendants of yellow and orange shades are often fragrant.Tulips are used to make wine .the tulip bulb and its petals are edible.The petals are chopped and combined with a salad. The petals help in adding color to the meal. However, it is not recommended to consume many tulips because the bulbs and petals have small amounts of toxic glycosides. petals tastes varies by variety and season, and is roughly similar to lettuce.Tulip bulbs look similar to onions, but should not generally be considered food.

Symbolizes

It was also associated with the House of Osman, resulting in tulips being widely used in decorative motifs on tiles, mosques, fabrics, crockery, etc , in the Ottoman Empire. The tulip was seen as a symbol of abundance and indulgence.The tulip is the national symbol for martyrdom in Iran
