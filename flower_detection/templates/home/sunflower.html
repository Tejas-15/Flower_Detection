Introduction

Sunflowers are giants among flowers.The leaves are wide, rough, and 3 to 12 inches.The sunflower ( Helianthus annuus ) is a living annual plant,with a large flower head (capitulum). The stem of the flower can grow up to 3 metres tall, with a flower head that can be 30 cm wide.These flowers follow the sunâ€™s movement by facing the east in the morning and the west at dusk, a behavior called heliotropism, they can easily grow eight to 12 feet within only six months.The bloom of a sunflower has two parts. The center is a disk of small flowers packed closely together,the disk flowers are brown, yellow, or purple.Other types of sunflowers include the California Royal Sunflower, which has a burgundy (red + purple) flower head. Etymology

The Latin name for sunflower is derived from the Greek word helios, meaning sun, and anthos, meaning flower; the second part of the Latin name for sunflower, annuus, means yearly.

Botany

1.) Evolution

Sunflower was domesticated 4000 years ago by Native Americans in Eastern North America.The sunflower was brought to Europe by Spanish explorers in the sixteenth century and from Spain the sunflower spread to Russia; today the sunflower is a common sight across the world and is cultivated in France, Spain, Italy, the United States, Russia, Argentina, India and Eastern Europe.

2.) Species

Russian Mammoth (Flower Height: 9-12 feet; Sun/Water/Soil: Full sun, neutral to alkaline soil),American Giant(Flower Height: 10-16 feet; Sun/Water/Soil: Full sun, neutral to alkaline soil, moist soil),Earthwalker(Flower Height: 7-9 feet; Sun/Water/Soil: Full sun, alkaline soils no greater than pH 6.5), Sundance Kid(Flower Height: 24 inches;Sun/Water/Soil: Full sun, neutral to alkaline soil, down to pH 5.5) There are number of sunflowers that grow to heights of only three feet or smaller because of increased hybridizing; Scientifically known as dwarf sunflowers, these plants love to grow in bunches and occupy small spaces such as gardens and planters.

3.) Medicinal uses

consumption of sunflower seeds leads to lower the risk of disease related to heart, high blood pressure.It contains nutrients that supports our immune system and boost the energy levels .sunflower oil :rich in vitamin E,good for heart health,strenthens immunity, also good for skin.The Native American Indians used the root of the sunflower in an infusion to treat snake bites . In traditional Russian remedies, the head of the sunflower and sunflower leaves were used to treat conditions such as bronchitis and rheumatism. cold-pressed sunflower oil used for therapeutic aromatherapy purposes).

5.) Perfumes and food

Sunflower seeds were a great food source for ancient South American cultures.Both the seeds and the oil of the sunflower are used in food preparations; sunflower oil is used in salads and as a frying oil . Sunflower seeds are a good source of nutrition and are high in vitamin D, vitamin B, niacin, and protein. Sunflowers can be processed into a peanut butter . In Germany, it is mixed with rye flour to make Sonnenblumenkernbrot (literally: sunflower whole seed bread), which is quite popular in German-speaking Europe.

Symbolizes

Sunflower is symbol of innocence and purity.The Aztec and the Inca cultures believed that the sunflower represented the sun and therefore worshipped the sunflower accordingly.

soil requirement is alkaline ,nutrient rich as well it blooms in summer to rainy if the seeds are sown between jan to june.To grow best, sunflowers need full sun. They grow best in fertile, moist, well-drained soil with heavy mulch. In commercial planting, seeds are planted 45 cm apart and 2.5 cm deep
